\begin{abstract}
Probabilistic models of protein evolution underpin computational biology and are crucial tools in reconstructing life's origins.
We derive and present novel theoretical results for evolutionary continuous-time Markov chain (CTMC) models, including
(1) closed-form expressions for expected sufficient statistics of the endpoint-conditioned birth-death-immigration process, via the score identity;
(2) complete- and observed-data approaches to maximum likelihood estimation of substitution and indel rates under the
Thorne-Kishino-Felsenstein (TKF) models;
(3) a hierarchcal TKF-extension that allows insertion and deletion of micro-domains (MixDom); and
(4) MixDom-based algorithms for parameter-fitting and inference.
\end{abstract}
