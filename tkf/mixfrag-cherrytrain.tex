\section{MixFrag Training from Alignment-Summary Counts}
\label{sec:mixfrag-cherrytrain}

The MixFrag Baum-Welch algorithm of Section~\ref{sec:bw-mixfrag} marginalises the
latent fragtype inside a per-pair Forward-Backward, so its per-fragtype counts
$F_\frag,E_\frag$ are posterior expectations that must be recomputed for every
cherry on every iteration.
It would seem that the cherry-count summarisation used for TKF92
(Section~\ref{sec:maraschino-main}), which precomputes a fixed tensor of
alignment statistics and then fits it by autograd independently of corpus size,
cannot apply, precisely because the fragtypes are latent.
In fact it can: the fragtype-augmented \mixfrag\ transfer matrices have a
rank-one off-diagonal structure that lets each pairwise alignment be summarised,
per discretised time, by a small set of count tensors on which exact EM runs in
time independent of the number of cherries.

\subsection{The fragtype-marginalised transfer decomposition}
\label{sec:mixfrag-transfer}

Fix a pairwise cherry alignment at (discretised) time $\evoltime$, written as a
column sequence $c_1\ldots c_L$ with each $c_t\in\{\mat,\ins,\del\}$.
Under \mixfrag\ (Section~\ref{sec:mixfrag-machines}) the indel likelihood of this
column sequence is a sum over the latent fragtype $\frag_t\in\{1,\ldots,\nfrag\}$
carried by each column,
\[
P_{\mathrm{indel}}(c_{1:L}) =
\sum_{\frag_1\ldots\frag_L}
\tkftrans^{\nfrag}_{\sta,\,c_1\frag_1}
\left[\prod_{t=1}^{L-1}\tkftrans^{\nfrag}_{c_t\frag_t,\,c_{t+1}\frag_{t+1}}\right]
\tkftrans^{\nfrag}_{c_L\frag_L,\,\fin}.
\]
Collecting the fragtype index into $\nfrag$-vectors and writing
$\notext_\frag = 1-\ext_\frag$, the column-to-column transfers are the
$\nfrag\times\nfrag$ matrices
\[
(\transwithin_a)_{\srcfrag\destfrag}
= \ext_\srcfrag\,\delta_{\srcfrag\destfrag}
+ \tkftrans_{aa}\,\notext_\srcfrag\,\fragdist_\destfrag
\quad(\text{within a run, } a\to a),
\qquad
(\transbetween_{ab})_{\srcfrag\destfrag}
= \tkftrans_{ab}\,\notext_\srcfrag\,\fragdist_\destfrag
\quad(a\neq b),
\]
with start row $(\tkftrans_{\sta a}\,\fragdist_\destfrag)_\destfrag$ and end column
$(\notext_\srcfrag\,\tkftrans_{a\fin})_\srcfrag$, where
$\tkftrans_{ab}\equiv\tkftrans_{ab}(\insrate,\delrate,\evoltime)$ is the TKF91
Pair HMM matrix of Section~\ref{sec:tkf91-machines}.

The off-diagonal transfer is \emph{rank one}:
$\transbetween_{ab}=\tkftrans_{ab}\,\notext_\bullet\,\fragdist_\bullet^{\!\top}$,
the outer product of the column vector $\notext_\bullet=(\notext_\frag)_\frag$ and
the row vector $\fragdist_\bullet=(\fragdist_\frag)_\frag$ -- the fragtype is
redrawn afresh from $\fragdist$ at every new link.
A rank-one factor cuts the transfer-matrix product, so at every change of column
type the chain separates and the likelihood factorises over the alignment's
maximal single-type runs.
Writing the runs as $a_1^{k_1}a_2^{k_2}\ldots a_m^{k_m}$ (with $a_i\neq a_{i+1}$
and lengths $k_i\geq1$),
\begin{equation}\label{eq:mixfrag-runfactor}
P_{\mathrm{indel}}(c_{1:L}) =
\tkftrans_{\sta a_1}
\left[\prod_{i=1}^m \runfac_{a_i}(k_i)\right]
\left[\prod_{i=1}^{m-1}\tkftrans_{a_i a_{i+1}}\right]
\tkftrans_{a_m\fin},
\qquad
\runfac_a(k) = \fragdist_\bullet^{\!\top}\,\transwithin_a^{\,k-1}\,\notext_\bullet,
\end{equation}
where the \emph{run factor} $\runfac_a(k)$ depends only on the run type $a$ and
length $k$.

\subsection{Match-run and gap factors}
\label{sec:mixfrag-factors}

Splitting $\transwithin_a=\diag(\ext_\bullet)+\tkftrans_{aa}\,\notext_\bullet\fragdist_\bullet^{\!\top}$
into extension ($\diag\ext$) and new-link ($\tkftrans_{aa}\notext_\bullet\fragdist_\bullet^{\!\top}$)
steps shows that a run of length $k$ is a concatenation of $p\geq1$ same-type
fragments of lengths $\ell_1+\ldots+\ell_p=k$, each an independent geometric draw,
separated by new links:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:mixfrag-mell}
\runfac_a(k)
= \sum_{p\geq1}\tkftrans_{aa}^{\,p-1}
\sum_{\ell_1+\ldots+\ell_p=k}\prod_{u=1}^p m(\ell_u),
\qquad
m(\ell)=\sum_\frag\fragdist_\frag\,\ext_\frag^{\,\ell-1}\,\notext_\frag,
\end{equation}
with $m(\ell)$ the \mixfrag\ fragment-length marginal (a mixture of geometrics).
In generating-function form, with $\Phi_a(z)=\sum_{k\geq1}\runfac_a(k)z^k$ and
$\mathcal{M}(z)=\sum_{\ell\geq1}m(\ell)z^\ell
=\sum_\frag\fragdist_\frag\notext_\frag\,z/(1-\ext_\frag z)$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:mixfrag-gf}
\Phi_a(z)=\frac{\mathcal{M}(z)}{1-\tkftrans_{aa}\,\mathcal{M}(z)},
\end{equation}
so each $\runfac_a(k)$ is a rational-generating-function coefficient, computable by
an $O(k)$ recursion.
A \emph{match-run} of length $k$ contributes exactly $\runfac_\mat(k)$.

A \emph{gap} -- a maximal block of $\ins$ and $\del$ columns -- factorises by the
same rank-one cuts into its alternating $\ins$- and $\del$-runs.
A pairwise alignment does not identify the relative order of insertions and
deletions inside a gap; summing over those orderings (as for the TKF92 gap-block
probabilities of Section~\ref{sec:tkf92-gapprob}) yields a factor that depends
only on the gap's deletion/insertion totals $(i,j)$ and its exit state.
Tracking deletions by $z_\del$ and insertions by $z_\ins$, let $P_\ins,P_\del$ be
the generating functions of gap prefixes (entered from a $\mat$ state) whose last
run is an $\ins$- resp.\ $\del$-run; they solve the $2\times2$ linear system
\begin{equation}\label{eq:mixfrag-gapprefix}
P_\ins=\bigl(\tkftrans_{\mat\ins}+P_\del\,\tkftrans_{\del\ins}\bigr)\Phi_\ins(z_\ins),
\qquad
P_\del=\bigl(\tkftrans_{\mat\del}+P_\ins\,\tkftrans_{\ins\del}\bigr)\Phi_\del(z_\del),
\end{equation}
and the gap generating function with exit state $Y\in\{\mat,\fin\}$ is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:mixfrag-gapgf}
\mathcal{G}_{Y}(z_\del,z_\ins)
= P_\ins\,\tkftrans_{\ins Y}+P_\del\,\tkftrans_{\del Y}
= \sum_{i,j}G_Y(i,j)\,z_\del^{\,i} z_\ins^{\,j}.
\end{equation}
The ordering-summed gap-block factor $G_Y(i,j)$ is recovered by the bivariate
recursion obtained on clearing the denominators of~\eqref{eq:mixfrag-gapprefix}.
The entry state is fixed to $\mat$ because the TKF91 rows $\sta,\mat,\ins$
coincide (Section~\ref{sec:tkf91-machines}): a gap entered from the start $\sta$
has the same factor as one entered from a match.

\subsection{Alignment-summary sufficient statistics}
\label{sec:mixfrag-summary}

Combining~\eqref{eq:mixfrag-runfactor} with the gap factorisation, every cherry
alignment enters the indel likelihood, per time bin $\evoltime$, only through the
following counts:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\Nmatch(k)$ -- the number of maximal $\mat$-runs of length $k$
  (factor $\runfac_\mat(k)$);
\item $\NgapM(i,j)$, $(i,j)\neq(0,0)$ -- the number of gaps exiting into a match
  (factor $G_\mat(i,j)$); by the row coincidence above this also covers
  \emph{leading} gaps (entered from $\sta$);
\item $\NgapE(i,j)$, $(i,j)\neq(0,0)$ -- the number of gaps exiting at the
  alignment end (factor $G_\fin(i,j)$); this covers a \emph{trailing} gap and, for
  an alignment with no matches at all, the entire match-free
  $\sta\to(\ins/\del)^+\to\fin$ path;
\item $\Nstart$, $\Nend$ -- indicators that the alignment begins, resp.\ ends, with
  a match (scalars $\tkftrans_{\sta\mat}=\tkftrans_{\mat\mat}$ and
  $\tkftrans_{\mat\fin}$), and $\Nempty$ -- the number of empty cherries (no
  columns at all), each the bare $\sta\to\fin$ path of weight $\tkftrans_{\sta\fin}$
  (degenerate, and absent from real corpora);
\item the substitution counts $\Nsub(a,b)$ -- aligned residue pairs at $\mat$
  columns -- exactly as in Maraschino (Section~\ref{sec:maraschino-main}), and
  fragtype-independent because the substitution model is shared.
\end{itemize}
The case $(i,j)=(0,0)$ is excluded from the gap counts because two matches with no
intervening indel form one longer $\mat$-run, already counted by $\Nmatch$.
With $\theta=(\insrate,\delrate,\{\ext_\frag\},\{\fragdist_\frag\},\exch,\eqm)$ the
corpus log-likelihood is
\begin{multline}\label{eq:mixfrag-summary-ll}
\log P(\theta)=\sum_\evoltime\Bigl[\textstyle
\sum_k \Nmatch(k;\evoltime)\log\runfac_\mat(k)
+ \sum_{i,j}\NgapM(i,j;\evoltime)\log G_\mat(i,j) \\
+ \sum_{i,j}\NgapE(i,j;\evoltime)\log G_\fin(i,j)
+ \Nstart(\evoltime)\log\tkftrans_{\mat\mat}
+ \Nend(\evoltime)\log\tkftrans_{\mat\fin} \\
+ \Nempty(\evoltime)\log\tkftrans_{\sta\fin}
+ \sum_{a,b}\Nsub(a,b;\evoltime)\log\bigl(\eqm_a\exp(\revsub\evoltime)_{ab}\bigr)
\Bigr],
\end{multline}
each factor evaluated at $\theta$ and bin time $\evoltime$.

\subsection{EM on the summary counts}
\label{sec:mixfrag-summary-em}

Equation~\eqref{eq:mixfrag-summary-ll} is an explicit function of the count
tensors, so training is exact EM on those tensors.
In the E-step, the expected complete-data sufficient statistics contributed by a
segment are read off the gradient of its log-factor by the score-function
identity~\eqref{eq:score-identity}: the birth, death and time-integral statistics
$B,D,S$, the link count $L$, and the per-fragtype fragment-extension and -end
counts $F_\frag,E_\frag$ (the latter also the expected number of type-$\frag$
fragments) contributed by each $\runfac_\mat(k)$, $G_\mat(i,j)$, $G_\fin(i,j)$ and
start/end scalar are the partial derivatives of its logarithm with respect to the
relevant natural parameters; the trajectory count $M$ and the observation time $T$
(the bin time times the number of cherries in the bin) are tallied directly; and
the substitution statistics $W,U,V$ come from $\Nsub$ as in Maraschino.
Weighting these per-segment expectations by the tensor counts and summing over
bins gives the corpus-level expectations.
The M-step is then the closed form of Section~\ref{sec:bw-mixfrag}: the
$\fragdist_\frag$ (categorical) and $\ext_\frag$ (geometric) updates from
$E_\frag,F_\frag$, together with the TKF91 $(\insrate,\delrate)$ quadratic and the
$(\exch,\eqm)$ substitution update.
After one preprocessing pass that builds the tensors, each EM iteration costs only
$O\bigl(\sum_\evoltime(\nfrag\,k_{\max}+i_{\max}j_{\max})\bigr)$ -- independent of
the number of cherries -- so an entire training corpus is summarised once and then
fit in time set only by the tensor sizes.
